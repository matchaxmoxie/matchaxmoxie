# Video script: How to Check-in with the iPads

**Canvas page:** How to Check-in with the iPads  
**Format:** Voiceover + B-roll (no talking head)  
**Target length:** ~2–3 min  
**Upload:** Kaltura → embed on Canvas (not public GitHub Pages)

---

## 1 — Hook

**VO:** When you arrive for office hours, start at the lobby iPad — before you head to your assigned office. Checking in here first helps front desk staff know you are on site and lets protégés know you are available. Those two minutes in the lobby mean a student is not wandering 300 North Eagleson trying to find you.

**B-ROLL:** Exterior or entrance of 300 N. Eagleson; cut to lobby iPad station (not the assigned office down the hall).

---

## 2 — Why check-in

**VO:** When you check in each shift, someone at the front desk can tell a protégé that their mentor is here — they are not left wandering the building. The iPad signals that you are available for them. It is a small step for you, and it makes a real difference for the people waiting on you.

**B-ROLL:** Mentor walks lobby → iPad; optional wide of FASE office area; quick cut to front-desk view if available.

---

## 3 — The taps

**VO:** It is three taps, the same way every shift — drop-in, then Marisa as the appointment type, then finish the on-screen steps. Marisa set this up so she can confirm who is here without leaving the desk. You are just running the same flow each week, and after a couple of shifts it becomes muscle memory.

**B-ROLL:** Screen recording or over-the-shoulder on iPad: drop-in → select Marisa (administrative coordinator) → finish on-screen steps. Marisa may appear in B-roll; no on-camera dialogue needed.

---

## 4 — After check-in

**VO:** Once check-in is complete, office staff and protégés can see that you are on site. Then you can head to your office hours.

**B-ROLL:** Hold on completed check-in screen; optional mentor walking toward assigned office.

---

## 5 — When and who to ask

**VO:** Please check in every office-hours shift — unless an SPC or staff member has told you otherwise. If the screen is confusing, Marisa or your SPC can walk you through it. Office staff are there to help with the process so protégés are not left waiting in an empty lobby.

**B-ROLL:** iPad station again; optional Canvas module sidebar highlighting Office Hours and Absence Protocol pages.

---

## 6 — Close

**VO:** Lobby iPad first, every shift — drop-in, Marisa. Small step, and it makes a big difference for the people waiting on you.

**B-ROLL:** Mentor enters office or sits at desk; fade on FASE branding or module page links.

---

## Recording notes

- **Tone:** Professional peer coordinator — engaging, kind, and clear; helpful without being casual or lecturing.
- **Mic:** Quiet room; pop filter or distance from mic to avoid plosives. Record VO separately from B-roll.
- **Pace:** Conversational, not rushed — ~130–150 wpm. Pause half a beat between numbered steps.
- **Music:** Optional low bed under B-roll only; duck or mute under VO. No music required.
- **Edit:** Match B-roll cuts to sentence breaks; let screen recording play through during Section 3 before VO continues.
