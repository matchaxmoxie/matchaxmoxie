# Video script: "I have an idea for an event!"

**Canvas page:** "I have an idea for an event!"  
**Format:** Voiceover + B-roll (no talking head)  
**Target length:** ~3–4 min  
**Screen share:** Canvas Files / module attachments for blank templates; sample pair on Sample Budget & Event Proposal Forms page  
**Upload:** Kaltura → embed on Canvas (not public GitHub Pages)

---

## 1 — Hook

**VO:** You have an event idea — a Lunch and Learn, a department collab, a partnership with another student org. Every FASE event started with a proposal like yours. Two forms, submitted together, give staff what they need to review it and respond.

**B-ROLL:** Event photos (tabling, L&L, FAB collab); GroupMe notification mockup → cut to Canvas module.

---

## 2 — Two forms, one package

**VO:** The event proposal and budget proposal go together as one package — staff need both to review your idea. Please complete both before you submit.

**B-ROLL:** Side-by-side PDF and budget doc icons; on-screen text: *Event proposal + budget proposal = one package.*

---

## 3 — Event types

**VO:** There are three types you can propose. Lunch and Learns — see the Lunch and Learns page for format and topic guidance. Collaboration events with other departments on campus. FAB events with other student organizations — but please get FAB approval before you bring the idea to FASE Office Staff.

**B-ROLL:** Module nav highlighting Lunch and Learns page; campus department or student-org collab photos; FAB approval note on screen.

---

## 4 — Where to find the forms

**VO:** Both forms live in Canvas Files or module attachments — same place for the event proposal and the budget template. The Sample Budget and Event Proposal Forms page has a filled example if you want to see what a complete submission looks like.

**B-ROLL:** Screen recording — Canvas Files, blank FASE Event Proposal Form PDF; scroll filled sample on Sample Budget & Event Proposal Forms page; highlight complete sections.

---

## 5 — What complete looks like

**VO:** I know the forms are a lot — please fill every section. No blanks, no TBD unless staff already approved a timeline for missing details. Be descriptive — what the event is, who it is for, date, time, location, partners, how it runs, food or accessibility needs. Someone reading your form should understand your vision without a follow-up email. On the budget form, list every expense with realistic costs — quantity, unit cost, total for each line. Link the actual item when you are ordering something specific. Line items should match what you wrote on the event proposal.

**B-ROLL:** Screen recording — scroll event proposal sections; zoom on budget line-item columns (qty, unit cost, total); optional vendor or catering link example.

---

## 6 — Submit together

**VO:** When you are ready, submit both forms together to FASE Office Staff — Canvas submission or email, whichever is live on the page when you watch this. Sending one now and the other later slows review, so please include both attachments at once.

**B-ROLL:** Highlight "submit together" on event-idea page text; email or Canvas submission mockup with both attachments.

---

## 7 — Close

**VO:** For a FAB event, get FAB sign-off first — then open both templates and complete them thoroughly. Event proposal plus budget proposal, one package. Thanks for putting the time in; your protégés will appreciate what you are building.

**B-ROLL:** Quiz module link thumb; SPC conversation B-roll; final shot of completed forms side by side.

---

## Recording notes

- **Tone:** Professional peer coordinator — engaging, kind, and clear; instructional without being casual or lecturing.
- **Mic:** Clear VO for form walkthrough — screen recordings carry visual detail, so VO can stay sentence-short.
- **Pace:** Slow down slightly during Canvas screen recordings; match VO to cursor movement in edit.
- **Music:** Optional minimal bed under B-roll; mute under screen share and VO.
- **Edit:** Use zoom/crop on PDF fields; pause on filled sample sections mentioned in VO.
