# Video script: Lunch & Learns (Video?)

**Canvas page:** Lunch & Learns  
**Format:** Voiceover + B-roll (no talking head)  
**Target length:** ~2–3 min  
**Status:** Page text draft ready — video optional; publish static copy first if you skip filming  
**Draft:** [lunch-and-learns.md](../lunch-and-learns.md)  
**Upload:** Kaltura → embed on Canvas (not public GitHub Pages)

---

## 1 — Hook

**VO:** Lunch and Learns let peer mentors offer academic enrichment — a topic from class, a service cause you support, a professional skill worth sharing. You lead the session with a faculty or staff collaborator. They are one of the main events you can propose.

**B-ROLL:** Past Lunch & Learn photos if cleared for training; students eating and engaging; mentor facilitating (no on-camera address to lens).

---

## 2 — What it is

**VO:** Students come, eat, and learn about a topic from you and a collaborating faculty or staff member. Keep it engaging — build in audience participation or an activity — and plan for about an hour.

**B-ROLL:** Room setup with food; mentor and faculty partner in B-roll; participation moment (discussion, activity).

---

## 3 — Good topic examples

**VO:** Strong topics leave students with something they learned — and a chance to participate, not just listen. Three categories work well: educational, service-related, and career-related. Examples for each are on the Lunch and Learns page. If you can find a faculty or staff member on campus with relevant expertise, partner with them on the session.

**B-ROLL:** On-screen list or page headings: good topic examples; Handshake or campus B-roll as texture.

---

## 4 — What to avoid

**VO:** Workshop-style topics are not a fit for this format — coding, arts and crafts. We want the audience involved, but we do not have the resources to facilitate co-learning like that here. If you have a workshop idea, propose it as an independent event instead.

**B-ROLL:** Page section "not a good fit"; contrast clip of lecture-style vs. participatory session (still photos OK).

---

## 5 — Sensitive topics

**VO:** Please steer clear of topics that get sensitive, divisive, or turn into a debate. Participation is encouraged, but all students should feel comfortable in our space. Lunch and Learns inform — they do not persuade or shame.

**B-ROLL:** Inclusive group shots; FASE space wide; on-screen text: *inform, not persuade*.

---

## 6 — Planning and proposing

**VO:** Plan Lunch and Learns during office hours — that is listed on the FASE Office Hours page. When you are ready to propose one, head to "I have an idea for an event!" for the forms walkthrough. Event proposal and budget proposal together, same as any other FASE event.

**B-ROLL:** Office Hours page excerpt — planning Lunch and Learns; cross-link highlight to event-idea page; optional iPad check-in or office-hours B-roll.

---

## 7 — Close

**VO:** If you have a topic in mind, check with an SPC before you open the forms — they can help you shape it before you start the paperwork.

**B-ROLL:** Mentor with SPC in casual conversation (B-roll only); fade on event-idea module link.

---

## Recording notes

- **Tone:** Professional peer coordinator — engaging, kind, and clear; focused on academic enrichment without being casual or lecturing.
- **Mic:** Record VO separately from event photos.
- **Pace:** ~130 wpm; let example topic lists breathe with matching B-roll.
- **Music:** Optional upbeat bed under B-roll only; skip if using event photos with ambient sound.
- **Edit:** Use page headings from [lunch-and-learns.md](../lunch-and-learns.md) as on-screen supers where helpful.
