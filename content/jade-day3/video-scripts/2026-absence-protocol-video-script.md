# Video script: Absence Protocol

**Canvas page:** Absence Protocol  
**Format:** Voiceover + B-roll (no talking head)  
**Target length:** ~3 min  
**Tone:** Reliability and trust — protect students and the team; same rules apply to in-services, MSLD events, and office hours  
**Upload:** Kaltura → embed on Canvas (not public GitHub Pages)

---

## 1 — Hook

**VO:** Class conflicts, exams, illness — they happen to all of us. When you cannot attend, protégés and the team need to know — not to catch you out, but because students rely on predictable support. This video walks through when and how to report an absence so no one is left wondering where you are.

**B-ROLL:** FASE office wide; mentor with protégé (B-roll); calm, professional tone — no scolding visuals.

---

## 2 — Priorities

**VO:** Your FASE commitments come first — in-services, MSLD programs, office hours, SPC meetings. Greek life, clubs, sports, and extra jobs fit around those. When something conflicts, plan around FASE if you can — or follow the absence steps we cover next.

**B-ROLL:** Module page thumbs — In-services, MSLD Programs, Office Hours, SPC Meetings; optional calendar with FASE blocks highlighted.

---

## 3 — Approved absences

**VO:** Certain situations count as approved absences. The full list is on the Absence Protocol page — please read it there. One note: the "other" category only counts with executive director approval.

**B-ROLL:** Simple on-screen list of approved absence types; hold each category long enough to read.

---

## 4 — Planned absence (24+ hours)

**VO:** For a planned absence, email the SPCs, executive director, and administrative coordinator at least twenty-four hours before the event. Include the event title, date and time, and your reason. The same advance-notice rule applies to in-services and office hours. For office hours specifically, follow the coverage steps on the FASE Office Hours page — including GroupMe to find coverage or switch shifts.

**B-ROLL:** Email mockup — To: SPCs, executive director, administrative coordinator; subject line; three bullets in body. Cut to Office Hours page coverage section; GroupMe coverage message mockup.

---

## 5 — Unforeseen (< 24 hours)

**VO:** If something comes up within twenty-four hours, notify the SPCs, executive director, and administrative coordinator as soon as you can. Late notice is still better than no notice — please send that email promptly so the team can adjust.

**B-ROLL:** Phone notification / email send animation; same To line as previous mockup.

---

## 6 — Consequences and cross-check

**VO:** Two or more unexcused or unapproved absences per semester means a meeting with the executive director. When you are unsure, email early and ask — a no-show leaves protégés without support. In-services, MSLD Programs, and Office Hours pages in this module all point back here.

**B-ROLL:** Link thumbs to related module pages; on-screen text: *When in doubt, email early.*

---

## 7 — Close

**VO:** If a conflict or illness comes up, please email as early as you can — your protégés and the team will appreciate knowing.

**B-ROLL:** Email compose screen with send tap; fade on Absence Protocol page in Canvas.

---

## Recording notes

- **Tone:** Professional peer coordinator — engaging, kind, and matter-of-fact; supportive without being punitive or casual.
- **Mic:** Record VO in one take if possible.
- **Pace:** ~125–140 wpm; pause after the approved-absence list before email steps.
- **Music:** Optional very subtle bed under B-roll only; none under VO or email mockup sections.
- **Edit:** Keep email mockup on screen through full VO in Sections 4–5; reuse same graphic for consistency.
