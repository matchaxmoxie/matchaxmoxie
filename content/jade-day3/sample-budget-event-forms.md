## Canvas paste (draft)

When you propose a FASE event, you must complete **both** the event proposal form and the budget proposal form. Submit them **together** as one package to FASE Office Staff (see ["I have an idea for an event!"](event-idea.md) for where and how to submit).

### Requirements for both forms

- **Complete every section.** Do not leave blanks or write "TBD" unless FASE Office Staff has approved a timeline for missing details.
- **Be descriptive.** Use full sentences and enough detail that staff can understand the event without follow-up email.
- **Submit as a pair.** An event proposal without a budget, or a budget without an event proposal, cannot be reviewed.

### Event proposal form

The event form should **explain the event completely and concisely**. A reader should understand:

- What the event is, who it is for, and why it supports FASE protégés
- Date, time, location (or virtual platform), and expected attendance
- Partners (departments, student orgs, FAB approval if applicable)
- How the event will run (agenda, activities, mentor role)
- Any accessibility, food, or safety considerations

### Budget proposal form

The budget form should list **every requested expense** with realistic costs. For each line item:

- **Link to the requested item** when you are ordering a specific product (URL to vendor page, IU catering menu, etc.)
- Describe quantity, unit cost, and total
- Note if an item is optional or required for the event to happen
- Align line items with what you described on the event proposal form
