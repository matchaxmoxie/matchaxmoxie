I approach student-facing and client-facing technical work as a practice of clarity, timeliness, and respect. Whether I am supporting mentees through mentoring services, leading an Agile client team at ServeIT, or shipping database workflows for research, my aim is the same: people should leave interactions understanding their next step and trusting the systems they rely on.

I am committed to educational and civic contexts where technology reduces administrative burden instead of adding noise. That has shown up in consolidating campus resources into accessible tools, automating repetitive advisor workflows, and helping students navigate study abroad and international applications with better-structured information.

Strong support relationships, in my experience, are reciprocal: I bring reliable documentation, predictable follow-through, and honest scoping; students and stakeholders bring goals, constraints, and feedback that should reshape the tool over time. I translate complex topics into actionable next steps so that ``I understood the meeting'' reliably becomes ``I know what to do on Monday.''

In research and product-style projects, I emphasize student and user agency---designing interfaces and data flows that make oversight and correction possible, and pairing ethical AI safeguards (bias checks, audit trails, educator oversight) with features people will actually adopt.

Collaboration across disciplines is not optional for the problems I work on. Health informatics, nonprofit redesigns, and clinic engagements have all required aligning engineers, subject-matter experts, and end users under shared definitions of risk and quality.

Accessibility and inclusion are not separate tracks from ``core'' engineering. Conducting audits, prioritizing WCAG-aligned fixes, and maintaining roadmaps for compliance are how I make equity operational rather than rhetorical.
