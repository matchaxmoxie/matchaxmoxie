I believe inclusive technology work extends beyond shipping features: it means questioning who is served by default workflows, who is left to adapt, and how we measure success when outcomes are uneven. My background combines implementation (full-stack and data systems) with accessibility practice and ethical AI safeguards so that ``compliance'' becomes an ongoing habit, not a one-time checklist.

In mentoring and program support roles, I have focused on reducing friction for students who navigate complex institutions---for example, consolidating fragmented campus resources into clearer digital tools and automating repetitive information-sharing so advisors can spend time on high-impact guidance. That work reinforced that belonging and clarity are technical problems as much as they are cultural ones.

I am motivated by purposeful action: turning audit findings into roadmaps, pairing transparent human oversight with automated systems, and choosing collaborations where accessibility and first-gen student access are explicit requirements from the start. If that orientation matches your team or program, I would welcome a conversation.
