Technology should shrink the distance between someone and what they need---not add a new maze because the team only user-tested the happy path. I spend time on accessibility and ethical AI because I have seen what happens when compliance is treated as a checkbox: first-gen students bounce off portals, municipal sites fail people in crisis, and ``smart'' tutors ship without a teacher in the loop.

My practice combines building (full-stack and data work) with audit discipline: Bloomington civic sites, Middle Way House, WCAG 2.1 AA/AAA targets, and research settings where HIPAA and human oversight are non-negotiable. I also mentor and automate advisor-adjacent workflows because belonging and clarity are often implementation problems.

I am looking for teams and programs that name first-gen access and accessibility as requirements on day one, measure what they ship, and treat ethics as engineering work. If that is you, I would like to talk.
