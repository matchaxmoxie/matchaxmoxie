\sectionboxed{How I think}

\begin{itemize}
    \item ``Never discourage anyone who continually makes progress, no matter how slow.'' --- Plato
    \item ``We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act but a habit.'' --- Aristotle
    \item ``Don't ever think, no matter how old you are, that you don't need to be coached.'' --- Peyton Manning
    \item ``Leadership isn't one person leading a team. It is a group of leaders working together, up and down the chain of command, to lead.'' --- Jocko Willink
    \item ``A true leader stays exhausted, abhors arrogance, and never looks down on the weakest link.'' --- (how I try to lead on teams)
    \item ``Wise are those who keep asking questions when everyone around them thinks they know the answers.'' --- J. Bartell
\end{itemize}

\sectionboxed{What I hold myself to}

\subsection*{Keep learning in public}
Feedback is not a threat to ego; it is fuel. I write things down so the next person is not blocked by my memory. Whether it is WCAG notes or a data pipeline handoff, I want ``understood in the meeting'' to become ``someone can run it Monday.''

\subsection*{Build for who is actually in the room}
First-gen friends decoding a portal, someone on a city website at midnight, a clinician behind a screen I helped wire---they all deserve patience and clarity, not vibes-only empathy.
