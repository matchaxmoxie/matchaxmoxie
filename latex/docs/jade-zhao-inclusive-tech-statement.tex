Tech should feel like someone scooted a chair closer for you, not like another maze because we only tested the happy path. I keep poking accessibility and ethical AI because I have watched first-gen friends bounce off portals, city sites fail people at night, and ``smart'' tutors forget a teacher in the loop.

I use modern AI and agentic coding assistants the same way I use any powerful tool: with disclosure when it matters, diffs reviewed by a human, and no pretend omniscience. The long version reads like a \textbf{product-style stack memo}: brief, stakeholders, scope, success checks, a SWOT-style risk register, vendor map, and how IU Luddy plus a big course-notes monorepo shaped my habits---on my hand-coded site: \href{https://matchaxmoxie.github.io/matchaxmoxie/\#how-i-work}{matchaxmoxie.github.io} (\texttt{\#how-i-work}).

My days mix building (full-stack + data) with honest audits: Bloomington, Middle Way House, WCAG targets, HIPAA-shaped research where humans stay accountable. I mentor and automate little advisor workflows because belonging is often just better information architecture.

If your team names first-gen access and accessibility on day one without making it a performance, I would love a coffee-chat style hello.
