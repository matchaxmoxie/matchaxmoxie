Visitors guide

Archival · Year '23 (2023)

Soft map for family and friends visiting Bloomington the way I framed it around 2023. Hours, road work, and dining change. Confirm before you drive.

  • Sample Gates · classic photo stop; expect crowds on football Saturdays and move-in.
  • IMU (Indiana Memorial Union) · food court, bookstore, lounges, fireplace energy; easy meet-up point.
  • Downtown Square · Kirkwood and the Courthouse Square for dinner; weekend evenings get busy.
  • Eskenazi Museum of Art · free museum time when open; check current hours.
  • Lilly Library · rare books; small bags; visitor rules matter.
  • Parking · campus garages and meters fill fast on game days; leave extra time.
  • Hotels · book early for Little 500, commencement, and football weekends.

Older IDS Source living guides from spring 2023 were useful snapshots. I am not linking Issuu here. Prefer current IU visit pages and your student's schedule.

Dorm notes

Archival · Year '23 (2023)

Hall vibes from tip sheets I kept around 2023. Communities, dining, and renovations move. Confirm LLC (Living-Learning Community) placement and amenities on the live RPS (Residential Programs and Services) site before you rank preferences.

  • Briscoe · Near the stadium; strong C-Store reputation; Civic Leaders community; bike lockers.
  • Collins · LLC energy, renovated gothic vibes, late-night café, near IMU and the Campus River.
  • Eigenmann (Eigy) · Own library and computer labs; a bit secluded; walkable toward Kelley and SRSC.
  • Forest · Tall building, strong dining hall; Media / Atkins LLC communities; elevators.
  • McNutt · Updated C-Store and dining; spacious rooms; single-stall bathrooms on halls; Starbucks nearby.
  • Read · X-shaped layout; Campus Café; music practice rooms; near Forest / Spruce / Willkie.
  • Spruce · Bigger windows and rooms; kitchenettes; short walk to Forest dining.
  • Teter · Luddy LLC home base; campus bus route; near Wright dining and the libraries.
  • Walnut Grove · Newer hall; near McNutt dining and tailgate fields; kitchens, laundry, study rooms, elevators.
  • Wells · Castle energy; historic women's hall; Goodbody Eatery; near science buildings and Mother Bear's.
  • Willkie · More private-bathroom options; short walk to Forest dining and Jacobs; own C-Store.

Elevators were common in Willkie, Spruce, Forest, Read, Eigenmann, Wells, McNutt, Briscoe, Walnut Grove, Union Street, Campus View, Tulip Tree, and some center buildings of Teter / Foster. Older halls like Wright / Ashton / Collins often meant stairs. Re-check before move-in day.

Freshman Welcome Week

Archival · Year '23 (2023)

TL;DR energy from community notes I curated in 2023 (including Reddit-era welcome threads), rewritten for a school-facing page. Move-in logistics age. Parties are not a how-to here.

  • Thank your people · say it out loud on move-in day. It matters.
  • Arrive with a plan · earlier arrival can dodge the worst traffic; not required.
  • Dollies help · especially if your hall is elevator-light. Confirm elevators for your building.
  • Floor events first · dorm / house activities beat staring at your phone all week.
  • Meet people while everyone is new · introductions get harder after week two.
  • Walk your class map · print or screenshot buildings before Monday panic.
  • IUware · grab software from iuware when you need it; welcome week is for humans first.
  • Travel in groups · leave and return with people you trust. Tell friends your plan.
  • Consent is non-negotiable · if someone cannot clearly consent, stop.
  • Know your limits · look out for friends; have a sober ride plan.
  • Hall rules are real · you agreed to them; RAs and campus police can enforce them.

I am not publishing 2023-era tips about hiding alcohol, fake IDs, or dodging enforcement. Stay safe. Stay kind. Verify Welcome Week schedules on current IU orientation pages.

Tips for college

Archival · Year '23 (2023)

  • Budget · track spending; use student discounts; thrift before you impulse-buy decor.
  • Textbooks · rent or buy used first; confirm the edition your syllabus actually wants.
  • Roommate basics · share fridge rules early; agree on quiet hours; you do not have to be best friends.
  • Bathroom kit · caddy + shower shoes. Future-you will thank present-you.
  • Credit load · many first semesters land around 14 to 16 credits. Ask advising before you stack hero mode.
  • Office hours · go before you are drowning. Professors remember people who show up curious.
  • Campus resources · tutoring, writing center, career services, counseling. Use them.
  • Storage · under-bed bins and a storage ottoman beat floor piles.
  • Command strips over nails · walls have feelings (and damage bills).

Outfit ideas

Archival · Year '23 (2023)

Pack light. Bloomington weather swings. Laundry exists. Year '23 formulas, not a dress code.

  • Oversized tee + leggings + sneakers
  • Hoodie + sweats + slippers (hall uniform)
  • Sweater + midi skirt + ankle boots
  • Button-down + chinos + loafers
  • Flannel + dark jeans + boots
  • Denim jacket + tee dress + sneakers
  • Blouse + trousers for presentations
  • Rain shell + shoes you can actually walk campus in

Stick to a color scheme, pack layers, wear your bulkiest coat on travel day. Going-out fits are your call; prioritize shoes you can walk in and a plan home.

College advice Q&A

Archival · Year '23 (2023)

  • Is IU a good fit? · Large public research campus, strong academics and arts, real athletics culture. Visit if you can. Fit beats rankings vibes.
  • How do I start Informatics early? · Try AP Computer Science Principles (AP CSP) if available, build tiny projects you can explain, ask questions in plain language. See the Class of 2027 guide.
  • What should I ask on a campus visit? · Class size, advising, housing costs, mental health support, career outcomes, and what students do on a random Tuesday.
  • Greek life? · Personal call. Price the dues and the time. Culture fit matters more than FOMO.
  • Changing majors? · Common. Talk to advising early rather than white-knuckling a path you hate.
  • Mental health? · Use official IU resources. I link them; I do not give clinical advice.
  • Parties? · Know your limits, travel with people you trust, look out for friends, consent always. That is the whole sermon.
