Logos (PB Method)

The PB sandwich exercise is a compact model of computer science reasoning: no hidden assumptions, no omitted steps, no ambiguous logic.

This site applies that theory as structural doctrine. Each year represents one layer of a larger algorithm with scoped inputs, ordered actions, and explicit verification.

This is not a generic timeline. It is a procedural map of how I build, mentor, recalibrate, and lead.

  1. Define inputs before execution.
  2. Sequence actions with precision.
  3. Declare assumptions explicitly.
  4. Validate edge conditions before release.
  5. Iterate from observed failure modes.

Across four academic years, one principle remains constant: reliability emerges from clear instruction and disciplined iteration.

Prognosis (Senior Year Projection)

The senior-year projection is derived from prior trajectory: rebuild under constraint, mentor through structure, and formalize what proves durable.

For that reason, emphasis falls on MAP coordination, capstone execution, and transfer of procedural clarity, not merely additional output volume.
