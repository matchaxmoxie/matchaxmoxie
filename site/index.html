方 protocol

the pb sandwich protocol

my engineering choices trace back to a classroom drill: write instructions to make a peanut butter sandwich. the professor literalized the gaps. spread nothing, because we never said to open the jar. the lesson stuck.

systems do exactly what you tell them, never what you meant. whether writing css, configuring canvas modules, or prompt-engineering models, clear instructions eliminate guesswork.

  1. understand your constraints audit what you are working with down to the bare parameters before writing the first line.
  2. enforce strict order do things in a logical sequence. misordered steps break system flow.
  3. declare assumptions say your silent assumptions out loud. leave zero room for interpretation.
  4. test the margins push boundaries and thoroughly test structural edges before declaring a feature shipped.
  5. leverage failure treat broken logic or edge-case failures as fresh technical inputs, never as a final verdict.
